## Design Context

### Users
Muslim students, remote workers, and founders who want structured productivity rooted in Islamic spiritual practice. They use Sayr during focused work sessions — not casually browsing. They understand Arabic Islamic terminology (nafs, muraqaba, dhikr) and expect it used correctly, not as decoration. They are seeking discipline through compassion, not hustle culture.

### Brand Personality
**Classical. Therapeutic. Candlelit.**

The feeling of a quiet study lit by a single candle — focused, warm, unhurried. Like a scholar's desk after Isha, not a Silicon Valley dashboard. Sayr should feel like it *slows you down* before it speeds you up.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Dark-only** — no light mode, ever. The darkness is the point. Candlelight-in-the-dark.
- **Gold (`#d4af37`)** is the primary accent — divine light (nur), not luxury branding
- **Emerald (`#2d6a4f`)** for completion and growth
- **Warm accent (`#c17f59`)** available for tertiary warmth — keep in palette
- **Glass morphism** with very low opacity, deep blacks (`#0a0a0f` base)
- **Arabic calligraphy (Amiri)** for spiritual anchoring, **Inter** for interface clarity
- **Geometric Islamic pattern** at near-invisible opacity — felt, not seen
- Anti-references: anything that looks like a generic SaaS dashboard, neon colors, gamification, streak counters, or productivity guilt

### Design Principles

1. **Absence of shame** — Drift is data, not failure. Incompletion is adjustment, not defeat. Never use red for errors or negative framing. The nafs is redirected, not punished.

2. **Spiritual elements are structural, not decorative** — Bismillah, dhikr, prayer anchoring, and Quranic/hadith references exist because the framework demands them. They are never cosmetic.

3. **Meditative minimalism** — Every element earns its place. Deep blacks and warm golds create calm focus. Whitespace is generous. Typography is light-weight. Nothing screams.

4. **Guided autonomy** — The six steps are sequential and intentional, but the user always has choice (finish early, start new session, skip optional fields). Guide without trapping.

5. **Warmth over precision** — Prefer soft glows over hard borders. Spring animations over linear. Off-white text over pure white. The interface should feel like candlelight, not fluorescent.
